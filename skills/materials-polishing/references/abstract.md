# Abstract Polishing

Use a six-part logic:

1. Engineering context.
2. Specific gap.
3. Material strategy.
4. Methods.
5. Key quantified results.
6. Bounded implication.

Do not open with broad infrastructure slogans unless the next sentence narrows quickly.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Overbroad opening

Before:

> Road construction is very important for national development, and emulsified asphalt has important significance in pavement engineering.

After:

> Emulsified asphalt tack coats are widely used to improve interlayer bonding in asphalt pavements, but their early-age strength and moisture resistance remain sensitive to curing and formulation conditions.

Why: the revision replaces a broad slogan with a specific material, function, and unresolved limitation.

### Example 2: Unsupported mechanism

Before:

> The waterborne epoxy proved the chemical bonding mechanism and significantly enhanced all properties of emulsified asphalt.

After:

> Waterborne epoxy increased the measured bonding strength of the emulsified asphalt system; FTIR and microscopy results should be reported before assigning this improvement to chemical bonding or phase-network formation.

Why: the revision separates measured performance from mechanism evidence and removes unsupported universal wording.
