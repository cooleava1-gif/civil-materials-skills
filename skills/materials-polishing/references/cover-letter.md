# Cover Letter Polishing

Cover letter logic:

1. Manuscript title and target journal.
2. Engineering problem.
3. Evidence-supported contribution.
4. Why the journal's readers care.
5. Compliance statement if supplied by the author.

Do not invent declarations, suggested reviewers, funding, or conflicts.

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Contribution without hype

Before:

> This paper is very innovative and will have important influence on pavement construction, so it is suitable for your famous journal.

After:

> This manuscript examines waterborne epoxy-modified emulsified asphalt as an interlayer bonding material and links formulation-dependent performance to curing, stability, and interface evidence. We believe the work fits the journal's interest in construction materials with measurable engineering performance.

Why: the revision states scope, evidence, and journal fit without flattering or overclaiming.

### Example 2: Compliance boundary

Before:

> There is no conflict of interest, no ethical problem, and all authors agree with the submission.

After:

> If confirmed by all co-authors, include the required declaration statements here: author approval, conflict of interest, funding, ethics if applicable, and data availability. Do not add these statements unless they have been verified.

Why: the revision prevents fabricating submission declarations.
