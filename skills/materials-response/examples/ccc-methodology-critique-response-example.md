# Example: CCC Methodology Critique Response

## Reviewer Comment

The manuscript discusses hydration products and microstructure, but the experimental method does not clearly describe curing age, sample preparation for SEM, or whether the XRD/TG assignments were supported by references. The mechanism conclusion is therefore not sufficiently supported.

## Good Response

We agree that the methodology description needed to be more explicit to support the mechanism discussion. In the revised manuscript, we have added the curing age, drying procedure, SEM sample preparation, XRD scanning range, TG heating rate, and the literature basis for phase assignments. We have also revised the conclusion so that it refers to `indications from XRD/TG/SEM evidence` rather than treating the mechanism as fully proven.

Revision made:

- Methods: added curing age, sample drying condition, SEM coating/preparation, XRD scan parameters, and TG/DTG heating protocol.
- Results: added a short assignment table linking each XRD/TG feature to its interpretation and supporting references.
- Conclusions: revised `the hydration mechanism was proved` to `the combined XRD/TG/SEM evidence indicates changes in hydration products and microstructure under the tested conditions`.
- Manuscript location: [insert page/line after revision].

## Why This Works

- It treats CCC-style mechanism scrutiny seriously.
- It strengthens method transparency before defending interpretation.
- It narrows the mechanism claim to the available characterization evidence.
- It gives the editor verifiable manuscript changes.
