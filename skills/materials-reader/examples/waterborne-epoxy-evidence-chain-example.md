# Example: Evidence-Chain Reading for Waterborne Epoxy Modified Emulsified Asphalt

## Input Scenario

The user provides a paper about waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt and asks whether its mechanism logic can support a short review on bonding performance.

## Expected Output Pattern

| Claim | Direct evidence | Mechanism inference | Boundary/limitation | Borrowable use | Reviewer risk | Citation role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Waterborne epoxy improves bonding strength. | Pull-off or direct shear bonding test with control group and dosage levels. | Improved cohesive network and interface adhesion are inferred. | Only valid for the tested emulsion, aggregate state, curing condition, and dosage range. | Use as performance benchmark. | Reviewer may ask for aggregate surface condition and repeatability. | benchmark/performance |
| Epoxy network contributes to improved stability. | Storage stability, viscosity, fluorescence microscopy, or curing evidence. | Network formation is plausible if morphology or chemical evidence exists. | Do not generalize to all epoxy emulsions. | Use in mechanism paragraph. | Reviewer may challenge mechanism if only strength data are shown. | mechanism |
| Material may improve pavement maintenance performance. | Laboratory bonding and moisture/aging retention evidence. | Service relevance is inferred. | No field validation unless field data exist. | Use as cautious implication. | Reviewer may ask for service-condition or field simulation evidence. | discussion |

## Good Reader Behavior

- Separate measured data from inferred mechanism.
- Extract dosage, curing condition, aggregate/substrate condition, and test method.
- Mark missing FTIR/SEM/fluorescence/rheology evidence as a limitation.
- Convert borrowable ideas into the user's own evidence needs.

## Bad Reader Behavior

- Do not write "the mechanism was proved" from bonding strength alone.
- Do not treat a figure caption as a conclusion.
- Do not ignore storage stability or viscosity when discussing emulsified asphalt constructability.
