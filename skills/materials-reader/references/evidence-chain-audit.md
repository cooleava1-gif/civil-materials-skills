# Evidence-Chain Audit

Use this when a paper will support a manuscript claim, review paragraph, journal-club critique, or experiment decision.

## Audit Ladder

| Layer | Question | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Claim | What does the paper actually claim? | exact claim, not paraphrased hype |
| Evidence | What measurement supports it? | figure/table/test/standard |
| Mechanism | Is the causal explanation directly measured or inferred? | direct / inferred / unsupported |
| Boundary | Where does the claim stop applying? | dosage, material, curing, environment, scale |
| Borrowability | Can the user reuse this logic? | yes / with extra evidence / no |
| Reviewer risk | What would a reviewer challenge? | missing control, missing mechanism, weak statistics |

## Materials Science Checks

For asphalt and emulsified asphalt:

- Separate binder/emulsion, interface bonding, mixture, and pavement-service evidence.
- Track dosage, emulsion type, curing condition, aggregate condition, and water stability.
- Treat FTIR, SEM, fluorescence, rheology, viscosity, storage stability, and pull-off/bonding tests as different evidence layers.

For cement/concrete:

- Separate fresh properties, strength, durability, hydration, microstructure, and sustainability boundary.
- Do not use compressive strength alone to support durability or mechanism claims.

## Output

Prefer a table with:

`claim | direct evidence | mechanism inference | boundary | borrowable use | reviewer risk | citation role`
