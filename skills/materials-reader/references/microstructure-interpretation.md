# Microstructure Interpretation Guide

Use this when reading or writing about SEM, fluorescence microscopy, AFM, DSC/TG, FTIR, or morphology figures.

## SEM Morphology: Asphalt and Polymer-Modified Systems

Common patterns:

- sea-island structure: modifier domains dispersed in asphalt matrix; often low-to-moderate dosage.
- co-continuous structure: two phases both show continuity; often linked to improved cohesion or compatibility when supported by performance data.
- phase inversion: originally continuous phase becomes dispersed; may occur at high polymer/epoxy dosage.
- cracks, voids, and debonding: possible brittleness, poor compatibility, or sample-preparation artifact.

Safe wording:

```text
The SEM images show a more continuous modifier-rich phase at intermediate
dosage, which is consistent with the observed bonding-strength trend.
```

Risky wording:

```text
The SEM image proves the chemical bonding mechanism.
```

## Fluorescence Microscopy

Interpretation cues:

- dark phase: often asphalt-rich matrix, depending on imaging mode.
- bright phase: often polymer/epoxy-rich or labeled phase.
- uniform fine domains: possible compatibility or stable dispersion.
- large separated domains: possible phase separation or storage-stability risk.

Boundary:

- qualitative images need image analysis before claiming domain size distribution or phase fraction.

## DSC/TG/DTG

DSC:

- Tg shift can support changes in mobility or crosslink density.
- curing exotherm disappearance may support curing completion.
- do not infer full network formation without complementary evidence.

TG/DTG:

- mass-loss regions can indicate water loss, decomposition, or hydration product changes.
- assignments require references or complementary tests.

## AFM

Asphalt AFM often shows:

- bee-like structures related to wax/asphaltene-rich domains.
- phase contrast differences after modification.
- roughness changes that may correlate with morphology but not directly with field performance.

## Reader Extraction Checklist

For each microstructure figure, extract:

- sample state and preparation.
- imaging mode and scale.
- observed morphology.
- linked performance result.
- mechanism claim made by authors.
- whether the figure actually supports that claim.
- safer wording for borrowing the idea.
