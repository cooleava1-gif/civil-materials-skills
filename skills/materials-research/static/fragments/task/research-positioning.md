# Task: Research Positioning

Goal: turn a broad materials interest into a publishable research question.

Default output:

- Research object: material system and application.
- Engineering pain point: what fails in service or current practice.
- Literature gap: what previous studies do not explain or validate.
- Design variable: what the study changes.
- Evidence plan: tests needed to prove the claim.
- Target contribution: performance, mechanism, durability, sustainability, or application.
- Best-fit journal family and risk.

Use a positioning table:

| Candidate angle | Required evidence | Best journal family | Main risk |

Strong positioning is narrow:

- Not "improve emulsified asphalt".
- Prefer "balance waterborne epoxy dosage, emulsion stability, and wet interlayer bonding for pavement maintenance".

For a master's project, prefer a topic that can produce one complete evidence chain over a grand topic that needs too many instruments.
