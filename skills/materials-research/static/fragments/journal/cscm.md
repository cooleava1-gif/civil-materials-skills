## Abbreviation caution

If the user writes `CCS`, confirm the intended journal when submission targeting matters. This skill provisionally maps `CCS` to Case Studies in Construction Materials only because users sometimes use informal abbreviations.

In routing, prefer `generic` until the user confirms `CSCM` or the full journal name. Do not silently assume `CCS = CSCM` for final submission advice.

## Best fit

Applied construction-materials studies, case-driven material applications, experimental evaluation of practical materials, regional resources, recycled/waste materials, and implementation-oriented work.

## Strong manuscript profile

- Clear construction-material application.
- Practical case, source material, or implementation context.
- Tests demonstrate suitability and limitations.
- The novelty is application, validation, or case evidence rather than fundamental mechanism.

## Weak fit

- Deep mechanism paper better suited to CBM/CCC.
- Pure pavement material paper may fit better in CBM in Transportation, RMPD, or IJPE.
- Manuscripts without a case/application dimension may look less distinctive here than in broader material-science outlets.
