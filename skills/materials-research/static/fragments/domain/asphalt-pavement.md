# Domain: Asphalt and Pavement Materials

Use for asphalt, bitumen, emulsified asphalt, pavement materials, tack coat, interlayer bonding, cold recycling, slurry seal, chip seal, micro-surfacing, and waterborne epoxy systems.

## Core logic

Asphalt/pavement papers need to connect:

1. Binder/emulsion design.
2. Storage, breaking, curing, and compatibility.
3. Interface or mixture performance.
4. Water/ageing/temperature/service durability.
5. Chemical or microstructural mechanism.
6. Constructability and field boundary.

## Key evidence categories

- Emulsion properties: particle size, zeta potential, viscosity, storage stability, demulsification/breaking behavior.
- Binder or residue: penetration, softening point, ductility, rheology, thermal stability.
- Bonding/interface: pull-off, shear, direct tensile, tack coat interlayer shear strength, failure mode.
- Pavement performance: moisture damage, rutting, cracking, fatigue, temperature susceptibility.
- Mechanism: FTIR, fluorescence microscopy, SEM, AFM, DSC/TGA, rheology, phase morphology.

## Waterborne epoxy emulsified asphalt branch

When the user studies waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt, load `references/asphalt-waterborne-epoxy.md` for deeper rules.

If the user provides data, also load `references/data-to-manuscript.md` to connect formulation, performance, figures, and result paragraphs.

Common reviewer risks:

- Epoxy curing is asserted but not evidenced.
- The emulsion is stronger but storage stability worsens.
- Adhesion improves in dry tests but water stability is not tested.
- Dosage selection lacks rationale.
- Microstructure images do not support the proposed mechanism.
- The paper ignores construction window, curing time, and compatibility with aggregate or old asphalt layer.
- The journal target is chosen before the paper angle is clear.
