# Output Templates

Use these templates only after applying the domain and journal fragments.

## Title patterns

- `Waterborne epoxy resin modified emulsified asphalt for pavement interface bonding: performance, durability and mechanism`
- `Effect of [modifier] on the [property] and [service performance] of [civil material]`
- `Balancing [performance benefit] and [practical constraint] in [material system]`

## Abstract skeleton

`[Context problem]. However, [specific gap]. In this study, [material/design] was prepared to [objective]. [Methods] were used to evaluate [properties/mechanisms]. The results showed that [quantitative finding]. [Mechanism evidence] suggested that [bounded explanation]. These findings indicate that [engineering implication], while [limitation or boundary].`

## Highlights

Use 3-5 bullets, each with one concrete message:

- Material design or method.
- Quantitative performance result.
- Durability or service-condition result.
- Mechanism evidence.
- Engineering implication.

Avoid:

- "A novel material was prepared."
- "The mechanism was discussed."
- "The results provide theoretical guidance."

## Cover letter paragraph

`Dear Editor, we submit our manuscript entitled "[title]" for consideration in [journal]. The study addresses [engineering problem] by [material strategy]. Compared with [existing approach/control], the work provides [main evidence-supported contribution]. We believe the manuscript fits [journal] because [scope fit], and it may interest readers working on [reader group].`

## Graphical abstract plan

Use a left-to-right flow:

1. Material components.
2. Modification/curing or interaction.
3. Performance tests.
4. Mechanism.
5. Engineering use case.

## PPT structure for a project report

1. Title and one-sentence problem.
2. Background and gap.
3. Material design.
4. Experiment matrix.
5. Main results.
6. Mechanism evidence.
7. Journal target and paper angle.
8. Risks and next experiments.
