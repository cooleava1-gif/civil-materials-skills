# Editorial Criteria

## CBM

Construction and Building Materials expects practical materials value, a complete experimental matrix, clear comparison with existing materials, and figures that support measured performance. desk rejection risks include narrow incremental contribution, no control group, missing test standards, and unsupported field-performance claims.

## CCC

Cement and Concrete Composites and related cement journals expect mechanism depth, composite logic, and novel insight beyond a property increase. Desk rejection risks include recipe-only studies, weak hydration or microstructure interpretation, and claims not supported by FTIR, XRD, SEM, TG/DTG, or rheology evidence.

## RMPD

Road Materials and Pavement Design, IJPE, and JRE expect pavement relevance, construction conditions, service conditioning, and compatibility with pavement materials practice. Desk rejection risks include no wet/aged/service condition, no standard test protocol, and no link to pavement layer function.

## JBE

Journal of Building Engineering and CSCM expect building or construction case relevance, application boundary, and often sustainability or practical implementation. Desk rejection risks include unclear building relevance, incomplete method description, or sustainability language without a quantified boundary.
