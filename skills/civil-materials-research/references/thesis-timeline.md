# Thesis And Paper Timeline

Use this guide to turn a broad civil-materials research goal into a realistic semester-by-semester plan. It is written for master's students who need one review paper, one experimental paper, and a defendable thesis direction.

## First 4 Weeks: Stabilize The Topic

Deliverables:

- One-page topic card: material system, target performance, test methods, mechanism hypothesis, target journals.
- 30-paper reading map: 10 reviews, 15 experimental papers, 5 standards or method papers.
- Draft review outline with 5-7 sections and a claim-source matrix.
- Preliminary experiment matrix with control group, dosage range, curing condition, and durability condition.

Decision gate:

- Continue only if the topic has a measurable performance target, a plausible mechanism evidence route, and enough recent literature.
- If the topic is too broad, narrow by material + performance + condition, for example `waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt tack coat bonding under moisture conditioning`.

## Months 2-3: Build The Review Paper

Deliverables:

- Literature matrix grouped by mechanism, performance, durability, construction applicability, and research gaps.
- Review manuscript skeleton: abstract, introduction, method/scope, 3-4 technical sections, future directions.
- Figure plan: mechanism schematic, evidence map, method comparison table, gap-to-future-work diagram.
- Reference audit: each major claim has recent primary sources, not only reviews.

Weekly rhythm:

- Week 1: search and screen.
- Week 2: read and extract.
- Week 3: make tables and figures.
- Week 4: draft one section.
- Repeat until all sections are drafted.

Decision gate:

- Submit the review only when the manuscript has a clear contribution beyond "summary": classification framework, evidence-chain map, test-method critique, or research agenda.

## Months 3-6: Run Core Experiments

Minimum experiment package for waterborne epoxy emulsified asphalt:

- Formulation: control, 3-5 epoxy dosages, dry residue basis clearly stated.
- Fresh/material properties: viscosity, storage stability, residue content, curing condition.
- Bonding performance: pull-off and/or shear adhesion, dry and moisture-conditioned states.
- Mechanism evidence: FTIR for curing/chemical signatures; fluorescence or SEM for phase morphology.
- Constructability boundary: viscosity/sprayability, curing time, storage stability, practical dosage range.

Quality gate:

- At least three replicates for main performance tests.
- Record failure mode, not only peak strength.
- Keep raw data, processed data, photos, instrument settings, and excluded-data reasons.

## Months 6-9: Convert Data Into Paper

Deliverables:

- Results matrix: figure/table -> claim -> mechanism -> limitation.
- Statistical summary: mean, SD, n, test method, significance where appropriate.
- Draft Results and Discussion in evidence-ladder order: performance, comparison, mechanism, boundary.
- Data/FAIR package: raw data, processed data, metadata, data availability statement.

Decision gate:

- If mechanism evidence is weak, downgrade language from "demonstrates" to "suggests" and add missing evidence as future work.
- If the optimal dosage is unclear, report a practical range rather than forcing a single optimum.

## Months 9-12: Submission And Revision Readiness

Deliverables:

- Target journal table with scope fit, article type, formatting needs, and reviewer risks.
- Full manuscript with polished abstract, highlights, graphical abstract plan, cover letter, and reference audit.
- Reviewer-response reserve: extra figures, raw data package, method details, standards mapping, and limitations paragraph.

Pre-submission gate:

- Every performance claim has control data and variability.
- Every mechanism claim has direct characterization or cautious language.
- Every sustainability claim has a functional unit or is framed as future work.
- Every figure is publication-ready or has a production checklist.

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

- Monday: define one concrete output for the week.
- Tuesday-Wednesday: execute experiment, reading, or drafting block.
- Thursday: convert work into a table, figure, paragraph, or dataset artifact.
- Friday: audit evidence gaps and plan the next week.
- Sunday evening: write a 5-sentence log: what changed, what evidence exists, what is uncertain, what blocks progress, what to do next.

## Red Flags

- Reading without producing matrices or paragraphs.
- Running experiments without saving raw data and conditions.
- Writing mechanisms before characterization evidence exists.
- Chasing a high-impact journal before the evidence package is strong.
- Claiming sustainability because a material is waterborne, recycled, or waste-derived without a functional unit.

## One-Semester Rescue Plan

If time is tight, prioritize:

1. Finish a narrow review paper with a strong evidence-chain framework.
2. Run a minimal but clean experiment matrix: control + 3 dosages + dry/moisture bonding + FTIR + fluorescence/SEM.
3. Write one experimental manuscript around a practical dosage range and honest limitations.
4. Package all data and figures early so revision does not become chaos.
