# Characterization Guide for Civil Materials

Use this reference when planning or describing FTIR, SEM, fluorescence microscopy, XRD, DSC/TG, AFM, or rheology tests. It is a practical execution guide, not a replacement for instrument manuals.

## FTIR

Best use:

- epoxy curing, asphalt oxidation, polymer modification, hydration-related functional groups.

Sample preparation:

- ATR mode is preferred for thin films, residues, and many asphalt/polymer samples.
- For asphalt residue, evaporate water or solvent under controlled temperature before testing.
- For waterborne epoxy emulsified asphalt, record whether the spectrum is from fresh emulsion residue, cured film, or aged residue.

Typical parameters:

- range: 4000-400 cm-1.
- resolution: 4 cm-1.
- scans: 32-64 scans per sample.
- background: collect before each batch.

Key peaks for waterborne epoxy/emulsified asphalt:

- ~3400 cm-1: O-H stretching.
- ~2920 and 2850 cm-1: aliphatic C-H stretching.
- ~1730 cm-1: C=O stretching, often related to asphalt oxidation.
- ~1600 cm-1: aromatic C=C.
- ~1240 cm-1: C-O-C ether.
- ~915 cm-1: epoxy oxirane ring; reduction after curing can support epoxy ring-opening.

Methods wording:

```text
FTIR spectra were recorded using ATR mode over 4000-400 cm-1 with a
resolution of 4 cm-1 and 32 scans per sample. The spectra were baseline
corrected before peak assignment.
```

## SEM and Fluorescence Microscopy

SEM preparation:

- dry samples consistently before imaging.
- sputter coat non-conductive samples with Au/Pt when needed.
- report accelerating voltage, working distance, detector mode, and magnification/scale bar.
- avoid comparing images prepared by different drying or fracture methods.

Fluorescence microscopy:

- use consistent exposure time and filter settings.
- report whether fluorescence comes from labeled epoxy, natural fluorescence, or image processing.
- use image analysis if claiming phase-area fraction or domain size.

Methods wording:

```text
SEM images were obtained at an accelerating voltage of [x] kV after
[drying/fracture/coating] preparation. Fluorescence micrographs were captured
using identical exposure settings for all formulations.
```

## XRD

Use for crystalline phases, hydration products, mineral fillers, and some cementitious reaction products.

Typical parameters:

- 2theta range: 5-80 deg for many cementitious systems; adjust to phase targets.
- step size: 0.02 deg.
- scan rate: report explicitly.
- sample: powder, dried paste, residue, or filler.

Risk boundary:

- XRD is weak for amorphous asphalt/polymer phases.
- Do not infer chemical bonding from XRD alone.

## TG/DTG and DSC

TG/DTG:

- report sample mass, atmosphere, heating rate, and temperature range.
- common heating rate: 10 degC/min, but use instrument/lab protocol.
- interpret mass-loss regions with caution and references.

DSC:

- report heating/cooling cycles, heating rate, pan type, and atmosphere.
- use Tg shift as supporting evidence, not standalone mechanism proof.

## Rheology

For asphalt/emulsion systems:

- report geometry/spindle, temperature, shear rate or frequency, equilibration time, and whether the sample is stable during testing.
- separate Brookfield viscosity, DSR frequency sweep, temperature sweep, and strain sweep.
- do not compare viscosity values measured under different shear rates without stating the difference.

Methods wording:

```text
Viscosity was measured at [temperature] using [instrument/geometry] at a
shear rate of [x] s-1 after [equilibration time]. Each formulation was tested
in [n] replicates.
```

## Reporting Rule

Every characterization result should answer:

- What sample state was tested?
- What instrument parameters were used?
- Which feature supports the claim?
- What claim boundary remains?
