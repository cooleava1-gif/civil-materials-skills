# Manuscript Paragraph Examples

## Use Case

Use this when a student knows the manuscript structure but needs paragraph-level models for abstract, introduction, and results/discussion writing in waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt or related civil-materials papers.

## Example Output Shape

### Annotated Abstract

```text
[Context] Waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt is used as a tack coat
material to improve interlayer bonding in asphalt pavements. [Gap] However,
the relationship among epoxy dosage, curing behavior, emulsion stability, and
moisture-conditioned bonding remains insufficiently clarified. [Approach] This
study prepared emulsified asphalt with 0, 5, 10, 15, and 20% waterborne epoxy
by dry residue weight and evaluated viscosity, storage stability, pull-off
strength, FTIR spectra, and fluorescence microscopy. [Results] The 15% epoxy
group showed the highest mean pull-off strength under the tested curing
condition, while the 20% group showed increased viscosity and reduced storage
stability. [Mechanism] FTIR and fluorescence results should be used to support
curing and morphology interpretation, not to overstate unverified chemical
bonding. [Implication] The results define a practical dosage window that should
be validated under field moisture and traffic exposure.
```

### Introduction Funnel

```text
[Problem] Interlayer bonding affects load transfer and moisture resistance in
asphalt pavement structures. [Material strategy] Emulsified asphalt is widely
used as a tack coat because it can be applied at ambient temperature and forms
a residual asphalt film after demulsification. [Existing work] Polymer and
epoxy modification have been explored to improve cohesion and adhesion.
[Bottleneck] Yet many studies report performance gains without simultaneously
addressing emulsion stability, curing development, viscosity growth, and
moisture-conditioned retention. [Objective] Therefore, this study evaluates
waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt using a linked evidence chain:
storage stability and viscosity for constructability, pull-off/shear tests for
bonding performance, and FTIR/fluorescence microscopy for bounded mechanism
interpretation.
```

### Results and Discussion Evidence Ladder

```text
[Data trend] Pull-off strength increased with epoxy dosage up to 15% and then
declined at 20%. [Control comparison] Compared with the control emulsion, the
15% group showed a higher mean strength under the same curing condition.
[Mechanism evidence] The FTIR spectra showed changes in the epoxy-related
bands, and fluorescence microscopy indicated a more continuous epoxy-rich
phase at intermediate dosage. [Trade-off] However, the 20% group showed
higher viscosity and poorer storage stability, indicating that excessive epoxy
may reduce constructability. [Boundary] These results support a dosage
dependent performance benefit, but field durability should be confirmed using
moisture, aging, and traffic-related exposure tests.
```

### Methods paragraph (materials and specimen preparation)

Before:

```text
Waterborne epoxy resin was supplied by Company A. The epoxy equivalent weight
was 210 g/eq. The curing agent was a polyamine. The amine value was 280 mg
KOH/g. Cationic emulsified asphalt was used. The residue content was 62%.
The penetration was 68 dmm. Five epoxy contents were 0, 5, 10, 15, and 20%.
The mixing speed was 3000 rpm. The mixing time was 3 min. The mixing
temperature was 25 degC.
```

Problem: this is a fact list, not a reproducible method. The reader cannot see the logic from material specification to formulation, preparation, conditioning, and testing.

After:

```text
[Material specification] Waterborne epoxy resin (type [X], epoxy equivalent
weight 210 g/eq, [supplier]) and its polyamine curing agent (amine value
280 mg KOH/g, resin-to-hardener mass ratio [X:Y]) were used as the modifier.
Cationic emulsified asphalt (residue content 62 +/- 1%, penetration 68 dmm
at 25 degC, conforming to JTG E20 T0651) served as the base binder.
[Formulation design] Five formulations were prepared with epoxy contents of
0, 5, 10, 15, and 20% by dry residue weight of the emulsified asphalt.
[Specimen preparation] The epoxy resin and curing agent were pre-mixed,
emulsified with [emulsifier type] at [X]% by mass using a high-shear mixer
at 3000 rpm for 3 min at 25 degC, and blended with the emulsified asphalt.
Pull-off bonding specimens were prepared by applying the modified emulsified
asphalt at a spread rate of [X] L/m2 on [substrate] substrates (surface
preparation: [sandblasting]). After curing at 25 degC / 50% RH for 7 days,
the bonded assemblies were tested per [standard].
```

Quality bar: organize Methods by logical block: material -> formulation -> preparation -> conditioning -> testing. State standards, dosage basis, temperature, humidity, time, application rate, substrate, and loading protocol.

## Quality Bar

- Each paragraph must label what is evidence, interpretation, and boundary.
- Quantified results must be replaced with real values before manuscript use.
- Mechanism sentences require direct characterization evidence.
- Field/application claims require durability or service-condition tests.
