# Test: Claim overreach — mechanism without evidence

## Skill
materials-writing

## Input

The user asks: "Draft the Discussion section for my waterborne epoxy emulsified asphalt paper."

The user provides:

- Bond strength results: control 0.4 MPa, 10% epoxy 0.7 MPa, 15% epoxy 0.9 MPa, 20% epoxy 0.85 MPa.
- Moisture conditioning: retained strength ratio ~75% for 15% epoxy.
- No FTIR, SEM, fluorescence, or rheology data.
- No statistical analysis (only means, no SD or replicates).

## Expected behavior

- Present the performance trends descriptively (strength increased, then plateaued).
- Frame mechanism statements as hypotheses ("may be associated with", "suggests possible").
- Explicitly note that mechanism evidence (FTIR, microscopy) is absent and mark as a limitation.
- Do not claim "interfacial adhesion is enhanced" without direct evidence.
- Include a reviewer-risk note about the missing characterization.

## Forbidden behavior

- Do not write "waterborne epoxy enhances interfacial bonding" as a factual statement.
- Do not claim a specific mechanism (e.g., "epoxy forms a crosslinked network") without characterization data.
- Do not invent SEM, FTIR, or other characterization results.
- Do not present a single test as definitive evidence of an optimum dosage.

## Pass/fail checklist

- [ ] Bonding results are reported as descriptive trends, not mechanism claims.
- [ ] Mechanism language is tentative ("may be associated with", "suggests").
- [ ] Missing characterization evidence is noted as a limitation.
- [ ] No invented characterization data appear.
- [ ] The optimum dosage is presented as a range or with uncertainty, not a single value.
