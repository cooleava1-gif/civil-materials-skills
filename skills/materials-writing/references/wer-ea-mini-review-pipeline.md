# WER-EA Mini-Review Pipeline

Use this reference when planning or drafting a mini-review on waterborne epoxy resin modified emulsified asphalt, WER-EA bonding performance, or related modified emulsified asphalt systems.

## Required output blocks

Every WER-EA mini-review planning output must include:

1. Research question.
2. Screening criteria.
3. Evidence matrix.
4. Review outline.
5. Paragraph skeleton.
6. Gap wording.
7. Reviewer risk.

The goal is to organize an entire mini-review, not only produce isolated paragraphs.

## Research question

Write one main question and 2-4 subquestions. A good WER-EA question should name:

- the material system,
- the performance target,
- the mechanism evidence layer,
- the engineering boundary.

Example pattern:

`How does waterborne epoxy resin modification improve the bonding performance and service-condition stability of emulsified asphalt systems, and what direct evidence supports the proposed curing, compatibility, and interface mechanisms?`

## Screening criteria

Define inclusion and exclusion before writing. At minimum:

- Include central WER-EA papers with explicit waterborne epoxy resin or waterborne epoxy curing systems.
- Include adjacent modifier papers only if they inform mechanism, test method, durability, or formulation comparison.
- Exclude performance-only papers from mechanism claims unless they also include direct evidence.
- Mark field/service-condition evidence separately from laboratory performance evidence.
- Record search strings, databases, years, and language boundary when available.

## Evidence matrix

Build the evidence matrix before drafting. Required columns:

- citation key,
- material system,
- WER dosage or formulation,
- test method,
- performance evidence,
- mechanism evidence,
- durability/service evidence,
- figure/table anchor,
- claim strength,
- review section role.

Use `references/table-system.md` and `assets/templates/table-system-template.md` when constructing the matrix.

## Review outline

Default section logic:

1. Background and engineering problem.
2. WER-EA material systems and formulation strategies.
3. Bonding and interface performance.
4. Mechanism evidence chain: curing, compatibility, morphology, rheology, and chemistry.
5. Durability and service-condition evidence.
6. Construction/application boundary.
7. Knowledge gaps and future work.

Do not organize the review as one paragraph per paper. Organize by evidence role and research question.

## Paragraph skeleton

Each paragraph should follow this skeleton:

1. Topic sentence: bounded claim.
2. Evidence grouping: 2-4 studies or source anchors.
3. Mechanism interpretation: only as strong as direct evidence allows.
4. Boundary: missing tests, limited conditioning, narrow material system, or uncertain field relevance.
5. Transition: connect to the next evidence layer.

## Gap wording

Use gap wording that is specific and reviewer-safe:

- `Current studies show improved bonding under selected laboratory conditions, but wet, aged, and service-condition evidence remains limited.`
- `The proposed compatibility mechanism is supported mainly by morphology and rheology observations; direct chemical evidence is still uneven across studies.`
- `Few studies connect emulsion stability, curing behavior, and interlayer bonding in one controlled formulation matrix.`
- `Field durability should be treated as a future validation target unless field or accelerated service data are available.`

Avoid empty gap phrases such as `few studies have reported this topic` without naming the missing evidence layer.

## Reviewer risk

Flag these risks before drafting:

- claiming field durability from dry bonding data,
- claiming mechanism proof from performance improvement alone,
- mixing SBR/WER or other modifiers without separating roles,
- ignoring dosage, curing, emulsifier, or asphalt source,
- comparing studies with incompatible test methods,
- writing sustainability claims without LCA/resource boundary,
- giving submission route advice without checking current journal facts.

## Handoff

After the writing plan is built:

- send source-specific uncertainty back to `materials-reader`,
- send missing references to `materials-citation`,
- send figure needs to `materials-figure`,
- send data-table needs to `materials-data`,
- send final language to `materials-polishing`.
