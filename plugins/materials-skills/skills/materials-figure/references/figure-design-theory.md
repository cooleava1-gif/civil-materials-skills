# Materials Science Figure Design Theory

Good manuscript figures use visual hierarchy and information hierarchy to protect the evidence chain.

## Information hierarchy

The reader should understand the conclusion path in this order: material system, test condition, measured property, uncertainty, and cautious interpretation. Put the main comparison in the strongest visual channel, usually position or length, not decorative color.

## Color and grayscale

Use colorblind-safe contrasts and confirm grayscale legibility. Control groups should remain calm and stable; modified or optimal groups can use warmer accents. Do not use red/green as the only distinction. In grayscale, every series should still differ by line style, marker, hatch, or label.

## Multi-panel composition

For multi-panel figures, align axes and panel labels. Put performance panels before mechanism panels when the story is "property first, explanation second." Keep `(a)`, `(b)`, `(c)` outside dense data regions and use consistent final-size font.

## Civil-materials claim boundaries

Mechanism arrows should be solid only when a claim is supported by direct data. Use dashed arrows or captions for hypotheses. Durability figures must state conditioning protocol, retention formula, and whether the result is laboratory screening or service validation.
