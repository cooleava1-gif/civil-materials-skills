# Materials Science Review Criteria

## Asphalt / Emulsified asphalt / Tack coat

Critical checkpoints:

1. Binder type and source must be specified.
2. Emulsifier type and dosage must be reported.
3. Epoxy resin specifications must include epoxy equivalent, curing agent type, and ratio.
4. Mixing procedure must report speed, duration, and temperature.
5. Pull-off, shear, or interlayer bonding tests must state substrate, curing age, loading rate, and moisture conditioning.

Common weaknesses:

- Storage stability tested but not connected to bonding performance.
- Only dry bonding tested, no moisture conditioning.
- Mechanism claimed from FTIR alone without SEM, fluorescence, rheology, or thermal support.
- Dosage range too narrow to establish optimization trend.
- No comparison with existing tack coat materials or standards.

## Evidence hierarchy

- Performance claim requires test standard, conditions, replicates, and error bars.
- Mechanism claim requires at least two complementary techniques.
- Durability claim requires aging, moisture, freeze-thaw, or service-condition conditioning plus retention ratio.
- Sustainability claim requires functional unit, comparison boundary, and quantified metric.

## Cement / Concrete

Hydration, pore structure, shrinkage, durability, and mechanical claims should match standards, curing age, mixture design, and microstructure evidence.
