# Waterborne Epoxy Narrative

For waterborne epoxy modified emulsified asphalt, the strongest manuscript narrative is usually:

1. emulsified asphalt is practical for tack coat construction but can suffer from insufficient bonding performance and moisture sensitivity,
2. waterborne epoxy may improve cohesive strength and interface bonding through curing and network formation,
3. dosage must balance bonding, viscosity, storage stability, and construction workability,
4. curing and morphology evidence are needed to support mechanism claims,
5. wet or aged conditioning is needed before durability language becomes reviewer-safe.

Useful keywords: demulsification, curing, phase compatibility, pull-off strength, shear strength, storage stability, viscosity, moisture-conditioned bonding, fluorescence microscopy.
