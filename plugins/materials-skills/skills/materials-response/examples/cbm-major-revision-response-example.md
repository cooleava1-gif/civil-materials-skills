# Example: CBM Major Revision Response

## Reviewer Comment

The authors claim that waterborne epoxy forms a stable network in emulsified asphalt, but no direct evidence is provided. The mechanism discussion is speculative and should be revised.

## Good Response

We sincerely appreciate the reviewer's insightful comment. We agree that the original manuscript did not sufficiently distinguish direct evidence from interpretation. In the revised manuscript, we have toned down the mechanism claim and clarified that the network formation is inferred from the observed bonding-strength trend and storage-stability behavior. We have also added a limitation statement noting that direct microstructural evidence such as fluorescence microscopy or SEM would be required to confirm the morphology of the epoxy-asphalt phase.

Revision made:

- The phrase "the epoxy network was confirmed" was revised to "the results suggest that the improved bonding performance may be associated with network formation during curing."
- A limitation sentence was added to the mechanism discussion: "Further microscopic characterization is needed to directly verify the phase morphology and network structure."
- Manuscript location: [insert page/line after revision].

## Why This Works

- It accepts the valid concern.
- It does not invent new FTIR/SEM data.
- It weakens the claim while preserving the contribution.
- It gives the editor a verifiable manuscript action.
