# Road Materials and Pavement Design (RMPD) & International Journal of Pavement Engineering (IJPE) — Formatting Facts

Authoritative facts about RMPD and IJPE formatting requirements. This file holds the **facts**; each skill's `static/fragments/journal/rmpd-ijpe.md` adds the **action layer**.

> Always verify against the journals' current guidelines before final submission. The numbers below are accurate as of early 2026.
> RMPD: https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/trmp20
> IJPE: https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/gpav20

## Article types and limits

### RMPD

| Article type | Word limit | Abstract | References |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Research article** | ~8,000 (including references) | ~200 words, unstructured | ~40 typical |
| **Review article** | ~12,000 | ~250 words | Comprehensive |
| **Short communication** | ~3,000 | ~150 words | ~20 |

### IJPE

| Article type | Word limit | Abstract | References |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Research article** | ~8,000 | ~200 words, unstructured | ~40 typical |
| **Review article** | ~12,000 | ~250 words | Comprehensive |

## Abstract

- Unstructured format for both journals
- ~200 words maximum
- Emphasize pavement relevance: what the material changes in road construction, maintenance, or service life

## Figures and tables

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Resolution (halftone/photo) | 300 dpi minimum |
| Resolution (line art) | 600 dpi minimum |
| File formats | TIFF, EPS, PDF, or high-quality JPEG |
| Color mode | Color online; grayscale in print (check current policy) |
| In-figure font | Arial or Helvetica, legible at publication size |

## References

- **Style**: Numbered, sequential by first appearance
- **In-text**: Square brackets [1], [2]
- **RMPD format example**:
  ```
  [1] A.B. Smith, C.D. Johnson, Title of article, Road Mater. Pavement Des. 26 (2025) 123–145.
  ```
- **IJPE format example**:
  ```
  [1] A.B. Smith, C.D. Johnson, Title of article, Int. J. Pavement Eng. 26 (2025) 1234567.
  ```
- Taylor & Francis journals; follow their reference style guide

## Mandatory statements

- **Data Availability statement** — required (Taylor & Francis policy)
- **Author contributions** — required
- **Declaration of competing interest** — required
- **Funding statement** — required

## RMPD vs IJPE targeting

- **RMPD**: material design, laboratory performance, road-material characterization, practical pavement-material development
- **IJPE**: material behavior connected to pavement engineering, modelling, design, performance prediction, service implications

## Common desk-rejection patterns

1. **No pavement context** — pure material study without road/pavement relevance
2. **Missing service-condition testing** — only standard lab tests without temperature, moisture, or aging conditions
3. **Generic polymer-modifier framing** — not showing what the material changes in road construction or maintenance
