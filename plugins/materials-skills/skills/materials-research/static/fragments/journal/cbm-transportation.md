# Journal: Construction and Building Materials in Transportation

Official page to verify before submission:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/construction-and-building-materials-in-transportation
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/construction-and-building-materials-in-transportation/publish/guide-for-authors

## Best fit

Transportation infrastructure materials, including pavement materials, asphalt/bituminous materials, road construction materials, durability, maintenance, recycling, and performance testing tied to transport service.

This is a strong target when the manuscript's main use case is road construction, pavement maintenance, interlayer bonding, cold-applied asphalt systems, recycled pavement materials, or transport-infrastructure durability.

## Why it matters for asphalt topics

For emulsified asphalt, tack coat bonding, cold recycling, slurry seal, micro-surfacing, or waterborne epoxy asphalt systems, this journal may be more directly aligned than broad CBM because the application domain is transportation.

## Strong manuscript profile

- The pavement or transport-infrastructure use case is explicit.
- Testing includes interface, moisture, ageing, temperature, or construction-process relevance.
- Mechanism connects to pavement performance or maintenance.
- Practical limitations such as curing, compatibility, and storage are stated.
- The introduction explains why the material response matters for transportation service rather than generic material strength.

## Weak fit

- Pure polymer chemistry without road-material application.
- Building materials, cement/concrete, or structural topics not tied to transportation infrastructure.
- Asphalt work that lacks pavement-service framing, construction process, or durability relevance.
