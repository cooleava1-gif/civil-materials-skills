# Journal: Construction and Building Materials (CBM)

Official page to verify before submission:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/construction-and-building-materials
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/construction-and-building-materials/publish/guide-for-authors

## Best fit

Broad construction materials research with clear engineering relevance, including building materials, cementitious materials, asphalt/bituminous materials, durability, repair, maintenance, and infrastructure materials.

For asphalt topics, CBM fit is strongest when the manuscript is about a construction material system and its performance-mechanism relationship, not only pavement design.

## Strong manuscript profile

- Material problem is clearly construction-related.
- Experiments go beyond single-property improvement.
- Mechanism evidence supports performance changes.
- Durability or service relevance is addressed.
- Figures/tables show a coherent evidence chain.

## Weak fit

- Pure chemistry without construction-material function.
- Isolated lab optimization without service context.
- Review manuscript without checking current invitation or article-type policy.
- Pure structural analysis, geotechnical/mining topics, or unbound road-base material work unless the construction-material contribution is explicit.
- Asphalt work framed only as pavement engineering may fit CBM in Transportation, RMPD, IJPE, or JRE better.

## CBM writing stance

Use practical engineering language. Novelty should be framed as a material-design or performance-mechanism contribution, not as "first ever" unless verified.

## Review caution

Before recommending a review article to CBM, verify the current Guide for Authors. Treat unsolicited reviews as high-risk unless the official policy or editor invitation supports them.
